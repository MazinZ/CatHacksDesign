FAQ

CatHacks? What's that? CatHacks is the first MLH supported Hackathon to be held at the University of Kentucky. Hundreds of hackers will travel from colleges around the nation to Lexington, KY in order to flesh out ideas in the field of technology. Who can attend? In order to attend CatHacks, you must be a student at a post-secondary university. We accept high school students on a case by case basis, but due to legal liability, we are unable to provide travel reimbursement if you're not in college.
What if I'm not that great at programming yet? All we require is a desire to learn and a drive to create. Sign up anyway! Spots are limited, but we'll see what we can do. How much does it cost? CatHacks is completely free to all accepted members. This includes meals for the weekend and any swag sponsors may bring.
What should I bring? You'll probably need some kind of a computer. Make sure you bring chargers for any devices you're bringing. In addition, bringing a sleeping bag and pillow will allow you to get some sleep if you can't stay up for 36 consecutive hours. Finally, make sure you bring any toiletries you'll want for the weekend, including a toothbrush, toothpaste, deodorant, and a towel. Will there be travel reimbursement? We will be offering a limited number of $200 travel reimbursements to hackers. If you need travel reimbursement, please indicate this on your application, and we will contact you to discuss details. Proof of travel costs, such as a plane ticket or gas receipt, is required for reimbursement.
